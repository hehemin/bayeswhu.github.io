Ch1 Overview

  • This text presents both traditional and new theories of industrial organization(io): the study of the structure of firms and markets and of their interactions.

Ch1 Overview

  • Introductory microeconomics analyzes idealized models of firms and markets; this text takes a closer, more realistic look at them, warts and all.

  • The study of io adds to the perfectly competitive model real-world frictions such as limited info, transaction costs, etc.

Models

  • There are at least two major approaches to the study of io.

  • The first approach, structure-conduct-performance(scp), is primarily descriptive and provides an overview of io.

  • The second, price theory, use microeconomic models to explain firm behavior and market structure.

SCP

Models

  • According to the scp approach, an industry’s performance (the success of an industry in producing benefits for consumers) depends on the conduct (behavior) of its firms, which, in turn, depends on the structure (factors that determine the competitiveness of the market).

Price Theory

Price Theory

  • Price Theory models analyze the economic incentives facing individuals and firms to explain market phenomena.

  • Two reasons for the shift to this approach are the recent availability of data at a more micro level and advances in price theory.

  • In recent years, three specific theoretical application of price theory have won substantial support—transaction cost analysis, game theory, and contestable market analysis.

Transaction Costs

  • Transaction costs are the expenses of trading with others above and beyond the price.

  • This approach aims to identify a set of environmental and human factors that explain both the internal organization of firms and organization of industries.

  • The key environmental factors are uncertainty and the number of firms.

  • The key human factors are bounded rationality and opportunism.

Game Theory

  • Game theory uses formal models to analyze conflict and cooperation between firms and individuals.

  • Competition among firms is viewed as a game of strategies that describe the behavior of each firm.

  • Game theory provides insights in games in which there are relatively few firms.

Contestable Markets

  • Markets in which many firms can enter rapidly if prices exceed costs and can exit rapidly if prices drop below costs are called contestable.

  • With few firms but easy entry and exit, the market is contestable and can have the properties of a competitive market.
